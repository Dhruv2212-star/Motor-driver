The schematic is divided into 2 parts for easier designing-  
1. Power distribution system
2. Logic system

1. **Block 1 – Power System**
Objective-  

The Power System is responsible for receiving power from the battery, protecting the circuit from electrical faults, filtering supply noise, and delivering a clean, protected power rail to the motor driver IC. Every component in this block contributes to either protection, power conditioning, or power distribution.  
| Ref    | Component               | Selected Part                   | Why this part was used and what role it plays                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| ------ | ----------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **J1** | Power Input Connector   | Phoenix Contact MKDS 1,5/2-5.08 | J1 serves as the entry point for the external power supply. A screw terminal was selected because it provides a secure, reliable, and vibration-resistant connection for battery wires while allowing easy installation and removal during testing or maintenance.                                                                                                                    |
| **F1** | Resettable Polyfuse     | Bourns MF-R250                  | The MF-R250 was chosen to protect the entire PCB from excessive current caused by short circuits or wiring faults. Unlike a traditional fuse, it automatically resets after the fault is removed, eliminating the need for replacement. It is placed immediately after the power connector so every downstream component is protected.                                                |
| **Q1** | P-Channel MOSFET        | AO3407A                         | The AO3407A was selected to provide reverse-polarity protection with almost no voltage drop. If the battery is connected correctly, the MOSFET conducts normally with very low resistance. If the battery is connected in reverse, it blocks current completely, preventing damage to the rest of the circuit. This method is significantly more efficient than using a series diode. |
| **R1** | Gate Pull-Down Resistor | 100 kΩ                          | The 100 kΩ resistor ensures that the MOSFET gate is always held at a defined voltage during power-up and power-down. This guarantees predictable switching behavior and prevents the MOSFET from turning on unintentionally due to electrical noise or a floating gate.                                                                                                               |
| **D1** | TVS Diode               | SMBJ15A                         | The SMBJ15A TVS diode was selected to protect the circuit from sudden voltage spikes and transients generated by motors, wiring inductance, or external power disturbances. Under normal operating conditions it remains inactive, but during an over-voltage event it quickly clamps the voltage to a safe level, protecting sensitive components.                                   |
| **C1** | Bulk Capacitor          | 470 µF, 25 V Electrolytic       | C1 acts as the primary energy reservoir for the motor supply. Motors can draw large bursts of current during startup or rapid direction changes, causing the supply voltage to dip. This capacitor supplies that short-term current, reducing voltage fluctuations and improving overall system stability.                                                                            |
| **C2** | Decoupling Capacitor    | 100 nF Ceramic                  | While C1 handles low-frequency voltage fluctuations, C2 removes high-frequency electrical noise generated by switching circuits and motor commutation. Ceramic capacitors have very low impedance at high frequencies, making them ideal for filtering fast voltage spikes that electrolytic capacitors cannot effectively suppress.                                                  |


2. **Block 2 – Motor Driver System**
Objective-  

The Motor Driver System receives logic commands from an external microcontroller and controls two DC motors using the TB6612FNG dual H-bridge driver. It also provides the required power conditioning for the IC and exposes the motor outputs through dedicated connectors.

| Ref    | Component                         | Selected Part                   | Why this part was used and what role it plays                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| ------ | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **U1** | Dual H-Bridge Motor Driver        | TB6612FNG                       | The TB6612FNG was selected because it is a reliable dual H-bridge motor driver capable of independently controlling two DC motors. It supports PWM speed control, forward/reverse operation, braking, and standby mode while offering significantly lower power loss than older bipolar motor drivers such as the L298N. |
| **J2** | Logic Interface Header            | 1×9 Pin Header                  | J2 provides the electrical interface between the motor driver PCB and an external microcontroller such as an STM32, Arduino, or ESP32. It carries all required logic signals including PWM inputs, direction control, standby control, logic power, and ground.                                                          |
| **J3** | Motor A Connector                 | Phoenix Contact MKDS 1,5/2-5.08 | J3 provides a secure connection between the motor driver outputs and Motor A. A screw terminal was selected to allow easy connection and replacement of motors while providing reliable electrical contact under vibration and higher current conditions.                                                                |
| **J4** | Motor B Connector                 | Phoenix Contact MKDS 1,5/2-5.08 | J4 performs the same function as J3 for Motor B, providing a durable and removable connection for the second motor output.                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **C3** | Logic Supply Decoupling Capacitor | 100 nF Ceramic                  | C3 stabilizes the logic supply (VCC) of the TB6612FNG by filtering high-frequency noise. This prevents disturbances from affecting the IC's internal control circuitry and improves overall reliability.                                                                                                                 |
| **C4** | Motor Supply Decoupling Capacitor | 100 nF Ceramic                  | C4 filters high-frequency switching noise present on the motor supply (VM). It is positioned close to the motor driver IC to minimize voltage spikes caused by the rapid switching of the internal MOSFETs.                                                                                                              |
| **C5** | Bulk Motor Supply Capacitor       | 10 µF, 25 V Electrolytic        | C5 provides additional local energy storage directly beside the motor driver IC. During sudden motor current demands, it supplies instant current to the TB6612FNG, helping maintain a stable VM supply and reducing voltage dips on the PCB.
