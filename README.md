The schematic is divided into 2 parts for easier designing-  
1. Power distribution system
2. Logic system

1. **Block 1 – Power System**
Objective-  

The Power System is responsible for receiving power from the battery, protecting the circuit from electrical faults, filtering supply noise, and delivering a clean, protected power rail to the motor driver IC. Every component in this block contributes to either protection, power conditioning, or power distribution.  
| Ref | Components                | Selected Part                   | Function                                                                                                                                              |
| --- | ----------------------- | ------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| J1  | Power Input Connector   | Phoenix Contact MKDS 1,5/2-5.08 | Provides the interface for external battery or power supply to the PCB.                                                                |
| F1  | Resettable Polyfuse     | Bourns MF-R250                  | Protects the PCB against excessive current by increasing its resistance during an overcurrent event. Automatically resets after the fault is removed. |
| Q1  | P-Channel MOSFET        | AO3407A                         | Provides reverse-polarity protection with a much lower voltage drop than a conventional diode, improving efficiency.                                  |
| R1  | Gate Pull-Down Resistor | 100 kΩ                          | Pulls the MOSFET gate low, ensuring the MOSFET turns on when the battery is connected with the correct polarity.                                      |
| D1  | TVS Diode               | SMBJ15A                         | Protects the circuit from voltage spikes and transients generated by motors or the external power source by clamping excessive voltages.              |
| C1  | Bulk Capacitor          | 470 µF, 25 V Electrolytic       | Stores energy and smooths large fluctuations in the supply voltage caused by motor startup and sudden load changes.                                   |
| C2  | Decoupling Capacitor    | 100 nF Ceramic                  | Filters high-frequency electrical noise that the large electrolytic capacitor cannot effectively suppress.                                            |

2. **Block 2 – Motor Driver System**
Objective-  

The Motor Driver System receives logic commands from an external microcontroller and controls two DC motors using the TB6612FNG dual H-bridge driver. It also provides the required power conditioning for the IC and exposes the motor outputs through dedicated connectors.

| Ref | Component                         | Selected Part                   | Function                                                                                                                                       |
| --- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| U1  | Dual H-Bridge Motor Driver        | TB6612FNG                       | Controls the speed and direction of two DC motors using PWM and digital control signals from an external microcontroller.                      |
| J2  | Logic Header                      | 1×9 Pin Header                  | Provides the interface between the external microcontroller and the motor driver IC. It carries VCC, GND, PWM, direction, and standby signals. |
| J3  | Motor A Connector                 | Phoenix Contact MKDS 1,5/2-5.08 | Connects Motor A to the AO1 and AO2 outputs of the TB6612FNG.                                                                                  |
| J4  | Motor B Connector                 | Phoenix Contact MKDS 1,5/2-5.08 | Connects Motor B to the BO1 and BO2 outputs of the TB6612FNG.                                                                                  |
| C3  | Logic Decoupling Capacitor        | 100 nF Ceramic                  | Filters high-frequency noise on the logic supply (VCC), ensuring stable operation of the TB6612FNG's internal circuitry.                       |
| C4  | Motor Supply Decoupling Capacitor | 100 nF Ceramic                  | Suppresses high-frequency switching noise on the motor supply (VM), reducing voltage spikes generated by the H-bridges.                        |
| C5  | Bulk Motor Supply Capacitor       | 10 µF, 25 V Electrolytic        | Provides local energy storage for the motor driver, helping maintain a stable VM supply during rapid motor current changes.                    |
